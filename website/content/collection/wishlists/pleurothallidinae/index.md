Cool-to-intermediate miniatures with **showy flowers**, picked to fill specific color gaps in the cabinet rather than chase species completionism. The Pleurothallidinae block is heavy on red because that's where the genus lives — the showy reds are Lepanthes/Pabstiella with flowers larger than the plant. The non-Pleurothallid picks (Dendrobium, Bulbophyllum, Aerangis, Erycina, Promenaea, Cadetia) bring in pink, violet, yellow, white, and bicolor — so the wishlist spans the whole color wheel at miniature scale.

**Size key:** mini-micro = whole plant <5 cm · mini = <10 cm · "small" = 10–15 cm and noted as borderline. Anything larger should not be on this list.

**Temperature key:** the cabinet runs 12–24 °C (cool, with cold-night spikes via the freezer); intermediate-cool growers fit too, in the warmer pockets near the lights. Species are tagged:
- **cold** = high Andean / NG highland 3,000+ m, wants 12–18 °C
- **cool** = cloud-forest 1,500–2,500 m, wants 15–20 °C
- **int-cool** = lower cloud-forest / mid-elevation Asian, tolerates 18–22 °C — fits warmer cabinet zones
- **intermediate** = 20–25 °C, fits the cabinet's daytime peak in the upper zone
- **⚠ int-warm** = wants 25–30 °C consistently — likely loss in this cabinet, demoted accordingly

## Adversarial pass — risks I'd weigh before ordering

I argued myself into this list; here are the holes I'd want a co-collector to push back on:

- **The reds are concentrated in Ecuagenera Europe.** If the box arrives with one or two losses, the red colour bracket of the cabinet takes a disproportionate hit. Hedge by picking up *Restrepia striata* + *Masdevallia ignea* from a German vendor as a backup.
- ***Lepanthes telipogoniflora* expected lifetime.** Famously short-lived — most growers report 18–36 months under good conditions. Budget psychologically as a flowering display, not a permanent acquisition.
- ***Pabstiella mirabilis* sourcing.** Brazilian — Ecuagenera EU may genuinely not have it. Better candidates if it's not there: **Schwerter Orchideenzucht** (sometimes carries Pabstiella), **Roellke Orchideen** (German Brazilian-orchid specialist).
- ***D. cuthbertsonii* — you lost the red form.** Take the lesson seriously: confirm placement (cool nights, high humidity, low intermediate light) before ordering pink/bicolor forms or you'll repeat the loss.
- ***Erycina pusilla* + *Bulbophyllum lasiochilum* are warmer-leaning** than the rest. They want intermediate-warm, not cool. If the cabinet doesn't have a warm pocket near the lights, defer or skip.
- ***Aerangis hyaloides* humidity vs. airflow conflict.** Ideal conditions are saturated humidity AND brisk air movement — easy in nature, hard in a cabinet. Decide if a dedicated mount placement above a circulation fan is feasible.
- ***Promenaea stapelioides* — Brazilian, intermediate.** Same warning as the *Masdevallia coccinea* 'Anchota' loss: warmer-edge species can fail in cool nights. Place mid-cabinet, not low.
- **Box-fee math.** Ecuagenera EU consolidated box is ~€40–50 flat fee. The 6-plant order above amortizes it; if half are out of stock at order time, decide whether to delay or substitute from the watchlist below to keep the box full.

## Cultural notes for the next order

- **Mounting:** Lepanthes, Pabstiella, Acianthera, Aerangis, Cadetia all want cork-with-living-sphagnum-pad — same setup as the Dracula mounts. Plan upper-zone real estate before ordering.
- **Light zones:**
  - Lower (Dracula zone): the cool/shaded growers — Lepanthes, Acianthera, Aerangis, Cadetia, Pabstiella, Stelis, Trichosalpinx
  - Mid: *Pleurothallis allenii*, *Restrepia striata*, *Masdevallia ignea*, *Promenaea*
  - Upper: NG Dendrobium and most Bulbophyllum (more light, faster drying)
  - Warmest pocket: *Erycina pusilla*, *Bulbophyllum lasiochilum* (intermediate-warm exception)
- **NG Dendrobium thermal note:** *D. cuthbertsonii*, *D. lawesii*, *D. dichaeoides* and the violet-blue mystery species all want the same cool-night regime as the existing *cuthbertsonii* 'Yellow'. Place near it to ride the same gradient.
- **Sequence:** finalise the Ecuagenera order first (largest, single shipment), then the Großräschener NG batch, then opportunistic Claessen + Bulbophyllum/Erycina pickups. Keep a slot in each shipment for a watchlist substitute if a primary pick is out of stock.
