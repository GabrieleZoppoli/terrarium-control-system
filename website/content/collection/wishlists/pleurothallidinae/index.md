Cool-to-intermediate miniatures with showy flowers — biased to fill specific colour gaps in the cabinet. The Pleurothallidinae block leans red because that's where the genus lives; Dendrobium, Bulbophyllum, Aerangis bring in pink, violet, yellow, white, bicolor.

Cabinet runs 12–24 °C. *Cold* = high Andean / NG highland 3,000+ m. *Cool* = cloud-forest 1,500–2,500 m. *Int-cool* = lower cloud-forest. Anything that wants 25–30 °C consistently is out.

## Cultural notes

- Lepanthes, Pabstiella, Acianthera, Aerangis, Cadetia → cork with living-sphagnum pad, upper zone.
- **Lower** (Dracula zone): Lepanthes, Acianthera, Aerangis, Cadetia, Pabstiella, Stelis, Trichosalpinx.
- **Mid:** *Pleurothallis allenii*, *Restrepia striata*, *Masdevallia ignea*, *Promenaea*.
- **Upper:** NG Dendrobium, most Bulbophyllum.
- NG Dendrobium (*cuthbertsonii*, *lawesii*, *dichaeoides*, the violet-blue mystery) → same cool-night regime as the existing *cuthbertsonii* 'Yellow'; place near it.
